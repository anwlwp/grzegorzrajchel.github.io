Research

My work is in quantum information theory, with emphasis on entanglement, quantum channels, and structured mathematical problems motivated by quantum computation.

Recent topics include absolutely maximally entangled states, certification of quantum Fisher information, low-rank simulation and distinguishability of noisy quantum channels, and quantum algorithms for generalized eigenvalue problems arising in the Schrödinger equation.

Research interests: multipartite entanglement, quantum correlations, quantum channels, quantum metrology, and quantum algorithms.

Academic background

PhD, Center for Theoretical Physics, Polish Academy of Sciences, 2021. Doctoral thesis on quantum combinatorial designs under the supervision of Karol Życzkowski.

The thesis received the National Center for Quantum Information Technology award for the best doctoral thesis in quantum information in Poland for 2021-2022.

Postdoctoral researcher at ICFO in Barcelona in the group of Maciej Lewenstein, working on multipartite entanglement, quantum metrology, and machine learning in quantum information.

Current affiliation: BEIT Quantum Algorithms.
